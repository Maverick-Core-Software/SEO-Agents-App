## 1. Facebook Reel Technical Requirements (2025-2026)

### 1.1 Core Video Specifications

As of June 2025, Meta unified Facebook video so that most new video uploads are published as Reels. Meta states Reels "will also not have any length or format restrictions," meaning any aspect ratio and duration is technically supported. However, **9:16 vertical (1080×1920px) remains the recommended format for optimal reach and engagement**.

| Spec | Recommended Value | Notes |
|------|------------------|-------|
| **Aspect Ratio** | 9:16 (vertical) | Full-screen mobile; any ratio accepted but 9:16 gets best distribution |
| **Resolution** | 1080×1920 px minimum | Higher (1440×2560) preferred for crispness; lower = blurry |
| **Duration** | 15-30 seconds optimal | Technically unlimited; 15-30s gets ~45% higher completion rates than longer videos |
| **File Format** | MP4 (preferred), MOV | H.264 encoding is "gold standard" |
| **Video Codec** | H.264, H.265, VP9, AV1 | H.264 most widely compatible |
| **Frame Rate** | 24-60 FPS | 30fps minimum recommended for ads; 30fps standard |
| **File Size** | Under 4GB (Meta ad spec) | 512MB max for some page uploads; keep under 1GB for reliability |
| **Audio** | AAC, stereo recommended | Original audio or Meta Sound Collection music; avoid licensed music not in Facebook's library |

### 1.2 Safe Zone for Text & Creative Elements

Meta consolidated Facebook Stories, Facebook Reels, Instagram Stories, and Instagram Reels into a **single unified 9:16 safe zone** as of March 2026. Key creative elements (text, logos, CTAs) must stay within the center-safe area:

| Zone | Percentage | Pixels (1080×1920) |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Top (avoid)** | ~14% | ~270px from top |
| **Bottom (avoid)** | ~35% | ~672px from bottom |
| **Left/Right (avoid)** | ~6% each side | ~65px each side |
| **Safe zone** | Center ~45% of height | ~976px tall usable area |

The bottom 35% is larger because it houses profile icons, captions, like/comment/share buttons, and CTA overlays.

### 1.3 Caption / Text Overlay Specs

- **Always include captions** — burned-in subtitles improve accessibility and engagement (many users watch with sound off)

- Use high-contrast text (white with black outline/shadow is standard)
- Limit on-screen text to 1-2 short lines at a time

- Font size should be legible on mobile — minimum 40px equivalent at 1080 width

### 1.4 Facebook Reel Algorithm Ranking Factors (2025-2026)

Meta's Reels algorithm has undergone significant changes. The October 2025 algorithm revision changed the hierarchy of ranking signals:

#### Primary Ranking Signals (2026)

1. **Watch Time / Completion Rate** — Still the foundational signal. Users who watch to the end (or rewatch) signal quality content. The October 2025 revision elevated **repeated viewings (rewatches)** above simple one-time completion.

2. **Shares** — Particularly DM-shares (sending a Reel to someone via direct message). Adam Mosseri confirmed shares are now weighted higher than likes. Shares indicate the content resonated enough to be forwarded.

3. **Saves** — When someone saves a Reel, it signals high-value content worth returning to. Saves and follows-from-Reels matter more than likes.

4. **Originality** — Meta explicitly stated (March 2026) that content filmed or produced directly by the page owner is prioritized in feed and Reels. Views and watch time for original Reels approximately doubled in H2 2025 vs H2 2024. Recycled/cross-posted content from other platforms is down-ranked.

5. **Meaningful Interactions** — Comments that generate conversation, not just emoji reactions. The algorithm favors posts that spark discussion.

6. **Freshness / Same-Day Content** — Same-day content receives ~50% more algorithmic distribution than older content (October 2025 update). Timely, relevant content is rewarded.

7. **Engagement Velocity** — How quickly a Reel accumulates engagement after posting. Early momentum drives broader distribution.

#### What's Weakened

- **Likes** — Now a weak signal for Reels distribution compared to shares, saves, and watch time
- **Hashtags** — Still play a role in discoverability but are less important than engagement signals
- **Follower count** — Reach depends on how people interact with content, not how many followers you have

## 2. Facebook Reel Content Strategy for Local Service Businesses

### 2.1 Best-Performing Topics for Trades (Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC)

Based on industry marketing research and trade-specific social media analysis:

**Top-performing content categories for electricians:**

1. **Before & After Transformations** — Panel upgrades, old wiring to new, dim/outdated lighting to modern LED. Visual proof of work quality. These are the single highest-engagement format for trades.

2. **Educational / "Did You Know?" Content** — "Why your lights flicker," "What 200 amps actually means," "When to call an electrician vs. DIY." Positions the electrician as an authority.

3. **Safety Warnings / Danger Content** — "This is what happens when you overload a circuit," "Signs your panel is a fire hazard." Fear-based content drives shares.

4. **High-Value Service Spotlights** — EV charger installation, generator install, panel upgrade. These are the services with the highest ticket value.

5. **Behind-the-Scenes / Day-in-the-Life** — Loading the truck, arrival at a job, the tools you use. Humanizes the brand.

6. **Community / Local Content** — Storm prep tips for DFW, local event sponsorship, neighborhood-specific service calls.

7. **Customer Testimonials (Short)** — 8-15 second clips of satisfied customers, ideally on-site.

8. **Problem → Solution Format** — "Customer called because X. Here's what we found and how we fixed it."

### 2.4 Before/After Content Performance

Before/after content is the **single highest-performing content type for trades businesses on social media**:

- Visual proof of work quality builds trust faster than any other format
- Panel upgrades, lighting transformations, and wiring repairs show dramatic visual contrast
- "Before-and-after photos of panel upgrades, EV charger installs, and lighting projects build a visual portfolio that works 24/7"
- Before/after Reels get higher share rates because they're satisfying to watch and easy to understand
- Works as both educational content (showing the process) and promotional content (showing the result)

3. **Process (3-5s):** Quick cuts of the work being done
4. **Result (1-2s):** Back to "after" with text overlay: "200A panel upgrade — Dallas, TX"
5. **CTA (1s):** "Need a panel upgrade? DM us for a free quote"

## 4. Reel Engagement Best Practices

### 4.1 Hook Strategies (First 1-3 Seconds)

The first 3 seconds determine whether a viewer watches or scrolls. This is the most critical element of a Reel.

**Proven hook frameworks for electricians:**

1. **The Danger Hook:** "If you see this in your electrical panel, call an electrician immediately." (Fear + urgency)
2. **The Cost Hook:** "A 200-amp panel upgrade costs less than you think." (Curiosity + money)
3. **The Contradiction Hook:** "Most electricians won't tell you this about EV chargers." (Curiosity gap)
4. **The Visual Hook:** Start with sparks, a dramatic before shot, or a time-lapse — movement stops the scroll
5. **The Question Hook:** "Why do your lights flicker when the AC turns on?" (Relatability)
6. **The Bold Statement Hook:** "Your electrical panel is a fire hazard if it was installed before 1990." (Shock value)
7. **The Before-Reveal Hook:** Show the stunning "after" result first, then flash to the "before"

**Meta's own guidance (Dec 2025):** Meta shared that hook and audio are the two key components that maximize Reels performance. The first 3 seconds must grab attention both visually and audibly.

- **15-30 seconds** is the optimal length for Facebook Reels — achieves ~45% higher completion rates than longer videos

- **The October 2025 algorithm revision** elevated repeated viewings (rewatches) above simple completion — so content that warrants rewatching (e.g., fast-paced, dense information, satisfying transformations) performs better.

### 4.3 Audio Trends and Sound Design

### 4.4 Text Overlay / Caption Best Practices

- **Always burn in captions** — Many users watch with sound off; captions increase watch time by 12% on average
- **Keep text in the safe zone** — Center 45% of the vertical canvas (see Section 1.2)
- **1-2 lines max on screen at a time** — Too much text = cognitive overload = scroll away
- **Use high contrast** — White text with black outline/shadow on any background
- **Text should appear in rhythm with audio** — Synced captions feel professional and increase completion
- **Hook text in first frame** — "3 signs your panel is dangerous" should be visible immediately
- **CTA text in last 2 seconds** — "DM us for a free quote" or "Call (XXX) XXX-XXXX"
- **Font size:** Large enough to read on a phone screen at arm's length — minimum 40px at 1080 width

## 5. Content Topic Improvement

### 5.1 Making Service-Specific Content Engaging (Not Boring)

The challenge: electrical work is technical and can feel dry. The solution is to frame every topic through a **human problem, not a technical procedure.**

**Transform boring → engaging:**

| Boring Topic | Engaging Reel Topic |
|-------------|---------------------|
| "200 amp panel installation" | "This 100-amp panel couldn't handle ONE Tesla charger — watch what we did" |
| "GFCI outlet replacement" | "This outlet almost electrocuted a homeowner. Here's what was wrong." |
| "LED lighting retrofit" | "Watch this 1990s kitchen transform with $200 in LED lights" |
| "Surge protector installation" | "One lightning strike destroyed $3,000 in appliances. Don't let this happen." |
| "Commercial wiring inspection" | "We found THIS behind the walls of a Dallas restaurant" |
| "Generator transfer switch" | "When the power goes out in DFW, this family doesn't even notice" |

**Key principles:**
1. **Lead with the human stakes** — safety, money, convenience, fear
2. **Show, don't tell** — visual proof > verbal explanation
3. **Create curiosity gaps** — "You won't believe what we found" (then deliver)
4. **Use the "one thing" rule** — each Reel should teach ONE thing, not everything about a topic
5. **Add stakes** — "This could burn your house down" is more engaging than "This is not up to code"

### 5.2 Storytelling Frameworks for Short Videos

#### Framework 1: Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS) — Best for 8-15s Reels

1. **Problem (1-2s):** State the problem visually + verbally — "Your lights flicker every time the AC turns on"
2. **Agitation (1-2s):** Make it worse — "That's not normal. It means your panel is overloaded. Overloaded panels start fires."
3. **Solution (2-3s):** Show the fix — "We upgraded this panel from 100A to 200A. No more flickering. No more fire risk."
4. **CTA (1s):** "DM us 'PANEL' for a free assessment"

**PAS is the most effective framework for short-form video** because it creates tension and releases it quickly, which drives completion and rewatch rates.

#### Framework 2: Hook-Story-Offer (HSO) — Best for 15-30s Reels

1. **Hook (1-3s):** Grab attention — "This is the most dangerous thing in your house right now"
2. **Story (10-20s):** Tell the story — show the problem, the discovery, the fix, the result
3. **Offer (2-3s):** Make the CTA — "We do free electrical safety inspections in DFW. Link in bio."

#### Framework 3: Before-During-After — Best for Before/After Content

1. **After (1-2s):** Show the beautiful result FIRST (hooks attention)
2. **Before (1-2s):** Flash to the ugly/dangerous "before" (creates contrast)
3. **During (3-5s):** Quick-cut the process (satisfying to watch)
4. **After (1-2s):** Return to finished result with text overlay (satisfaction)
5. **CTA (1s):** Call to action

#### Framework 4: Listicle / Tips — Best for Educational Content

1. **Hook (1-2s):** "3 signs your electrical panel needs replacing"
2. **Tip 1 (2-3s):** Sign #1 with visual
3. **Tip 2 (2-3s):** Sign #2 with visual
4. **Tip 3 (2-3s):** Sign #3 with visual
5. **CTA (1-2s):** "See any of these? Call us before it's too late."

### 6.3 A/B Testing Topics

Meta provides a built-in **A/B content testing tool** in Meta Business Suite that allows testing different versions of Reels against each other:

**How to A/B test Reels:**
1. Create two variants of a Reel with **one variable changed** (different hook, different topic, different duration, different CTA, different audio)
2. Use Meta Business Suite → Content → A/B Testing to run the test
3. Meta distributes both versions to different audience segments
4. After the test period (typically 7-14 days), Meta declares a winner based on your chosen metric (reach, watch time, engagement)

**What to A/B test for Grizzly:**

- **CTA test:** "DM us" vs. "Call us" vs. "Link in bio" — which generates more leads?
- **Audio test:** Trending sound vs. original voiceover — which gets more reach?

**Testing cadence:** Run one A/B test per week (aligned with the 3-Reel-per-week schedule). Test one variable at a time for clean results.
